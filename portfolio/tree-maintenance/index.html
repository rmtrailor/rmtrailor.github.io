311 Tree Maintenance Calls in San Francisco 2015

Author: Rubin Trailor

Description

This visualization is a Choropleth Map detailing 311 Tree Maintenance calls in San Francisco from 2015. The colors of the area depict the the total number of reports for that neighborhood. Neighborhoods whose data wasn't available in the dataset has a grey color. Each circle is a 311 call at its exact location. Every circle is encoded in two colors: red or blue. Blue circles are calls that have been resolved(closed) and red circles are calls that weren't resolved(open)â€“at least within the time span of the dataset. It's also possible that these calls were closed but weren't documented. Unfortunately, this cannot be determined from the dataset. There is a group of radio buttons to toggle the visibility of these circles. Simply choose whether you want to see all the circles(default option), only the open calls, only the closed calls, or none of circles.

Findings

The Mission neighborhood and its surrounding neighborhoods seem to have a high amounts of these calls. After a close analysis of these calls, it became apparent that most of these calls were reports of vandalism to the trees. The Mission neighborhood is known infamously for its high concentration of graffiti, so this might be connection for the high ammount of calls for that area. This area also has the highest amount of calls that are still open. Since a lot of graffiti is kept up in that neighborhood, this might explain why some "vandalism" on trees might go unresolved.
